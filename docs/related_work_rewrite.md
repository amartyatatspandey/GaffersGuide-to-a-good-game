## Section II-D: Large Language Models in Sports Analytics

The application of Large Language Models (LLMs) to sports analytics remains nascent, building upon foundational transformer architectures \cite{vaswani2017attention} and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) methodologies \cite{lewis2020rag}. Historically, spatiotemporal analysis of team sports was restricted to statistical modeling of player trajectories \cite{gudmundsson2017}. Recent efforts between 2022 and 2025 have explored LLM-based narrative generation, producing automated sports commentary and match reports directly from event streams to enhance fan engagement \cite{author2023commentary}.

These models translate raw match events into natural language narratives, yet they frequently lack deep tactical grounding. Concurrently, RAG has emerged as a key mechanism for retrieving expert domain knowledge within high-stakes, knowledge-dense fields such as clinical medicine, legal analysis, and elite sports science \cite{author2024ragdomain}.
% [FIND: Paper on RAG in domain-specific expert knowledge retrieval (e.g., medicine or law), 2023-2024]
By coupling parametric model knowledge with non-parametric databases containing tactical manuals, these setups ensure factual compliance. Within the domain of sports coaching, initial systems have been proposed to serve as tactical assistants, leveraging LLMs to generate player performance recommendations and suggest defensive positioning shifts \cite{author2023coaching}.
% [FIND: Paper on LLM/Generative AI for tactical decision support or football coaching recommendations, 2023-2024]
Nonetheless, a primary obstacle in deploying these models in competitive coaching is the prevalence of spatial and statistical hallucinations, wherein an LLM fabricates match statistics or player positions. State-of-the-art systems mitigate this through the use of semantic guardrails, real-time confidence scores, and structured grounding techniques \cite{author2024guardrails}.
% [FIND: Paper on LLM hallucination mitigation, prompt guardrails, or domain grounding, 2023-2025]
These guardrails help align outputs with domain-specific rules, yet they often fail when processing dynamic spatiotemporal telemetry. Despite these advancements, a significant gap remains in the literature. To our knowledge, no prior work combines telemetry-first prompt constraints, deterministic ball-data guardrails, and RAG-grounded LLM routing in a real-time football spatial analytics pipeline.

## Section II-E: Positioning of Gaffer's Guide

To properly position our proposed framework, Gaffer's Guide must be contrasted with prevailing commercial systems and public academic benchmarks. Traditional commercial football analytics platforms, such as Tracab, rely on complex, multi-camera optical tracking arrays permanently installed inside stadium infrastructures. While highly accurate, these proprietary installations command prohibitive acquisition and maintenance costs exceeding $100,000, making them accessible only to elite top-tier clubs. Alternative commercial services, including Opta and StatsBomb, deliver detailed event-level data but rely heavily on manual human annotation augmented by proprietary computer vision techniques. Consequently, lower-league clubs and academy divisions lack access to spatial tracking systems. To address these data scarcity issues, public benchmarks like SoccerNet \cite{giancola2022soccernet}

have been introduced. However, these benchmarks focus on isolated tasks such as tracking or action spotting, leaving a significant gap in integrated tactical reasoning. Existing open-source academic tools typically target standalone components like player detection or tracking, failing to unify these into a functional coaching pipeline. Gaffer's Guide addresses these shortcomings by serving as a unified system that extracts near-commercial-grade spatiotemporal tracking data and produces LLM-grounded coaching intelligence directly from a single standard broadcast MP4 video file. This represents a distinct paradigm shift, enabling cost-effective tactical analysis for sub-elite organizations.
